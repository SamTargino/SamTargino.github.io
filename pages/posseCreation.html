The Game Equipment List Posse Creator

Posse Creation Rules

In order to create a Posse, each player will first:

Define the number of models per posse

Define the posse’s ability

Create Models

Define each model’s equipment

Defining Number of Models

To define the number of models, simply agree with the other player(s). Define how many models each posse will have.

The game is designed for posses of anywhere between 2 and 5 models.

Then define an amount of money each team will get to buy equipment for their models. Anywhere between $60 and $100 per model is a good idea. This money will be pooled for the whole Posse.

Meaning that a member may have $80 worth of equipment while the other $40 worth of equipment for a 2 man is worth $60 per model.

Defining a Posse’s Ability

When creating a posse, choose a posse ability from the posse ability list. These are shared by every member of the posse, and modify how the posse interacts with the game’s core mechanics.

Creating Models

Pick an ability for the model. Give them a name. Establish the model’s three primary statistics.

Unless otherwise noted, each model has an Ability Value of 12, an Injury Value of 3, and a Mortal Value of 12.

Equip equipment cards to each of your models. To do so spend the available on money for each item equipped. Each model’s equipment must be distributed along each model’s inventory slots, obeying the limits based on equipment sizes.

A model should start with at least one piece of equipment in their Held slot and most likely equipment in their Quick Slot, as well as equipment in their backpack.

It is highly recommended for every model to have at least a ranged weapon of some sort and a medkit. MODEL FACING

Models have a front and a back facing. This is defined by a straight imaginary line drawn through the center of the model along the base’s diameter (if it is a round base). Every object at the line and forwards is on the model’s front facing. Every object behind the line is on the model’s back facing.

THE CARDS

Equipment Cards

Equipment cards all have three primary features. Price, Size, Name, Keywords. All equipment has all these features. But many have more than these.

Price:

How much the equipment costs.

Size:

How much space the equipment takes up in the inventory.

Name:

What the equipment is.

Keywords:

Features associated to the equipment

Weapon Cards

Weapon cards are equipment cards and are the primary way a model has to injure and possibly incapacitate other models.

There are two types of weapons: ranged and melee weapons.

Although all weapon cards have damage, some tools and upgrades also have assigned damage values, despite not being considered weapon cards.

Damage:

The die type and modifier used when forcing another model to make a damage check.

Ranged Weapons

Ranged weapons allow the model to perform a shoot action whenever the model takes a use held object action. Ranged weapons also have the following attributes:

Ammo Capacity:

The maximum number of ammo tokens the equipment can have at any given time.

Reload Speed:

How many tokens can be added with every reload action.

Fire Rate:

How many times this item can be used each time it is activated.

Recoil:

The penalty applied to tough shot tests taken past the first shot in a single activation.

Shooting into distance penalty:

The penalty applied to tough shot tests taken within certain distance intervals past 8”. The intervals are 8 to 12, 12 to 16, 20 to 24, and greater than 24.

Melee Weapons

These are weapons used during the melee phase. Their two primary features are damage and size.

Tool Cards

These are cards that allow players to perform different actions when used. These are described under Special. Examples are dynamite and bandages.

Upgrade Cards

These cards can be used when equipping a model to modify a tool or weapon they may carry to do one thing or another. They are placed under the card they modify in such a way that their special text box.

Keywords:

Descriptive words on the card related to its effect.
